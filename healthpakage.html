What is the focus of the Well Woman Panel?
This panel is designed to monitor women’s health, including reproductive health, hormone levels, and screening for common female cancers.
What tests are typically included in the Menopause Profile?
The Menopause Profile usually includes tests for hormone levels such as estrogen, progesterone, and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), as well as assessments for bone density and cardiovascular health.
How can a Hepatitis Profile be beneficial?
This profile tests for different types of hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, etc.) and helps diagnose hepatitis infections, monitor liver function, and guide treatment.
What additional tests are included in the Extended Diabetic Profile?
The Extended Profile may include more detailed tests such as HbA1c, kidney function tests, and lipid profiles to provide a comprehensive view of diabetes management.
What tests are included in the Antenatal Profile?
The antenatal profile typically includes tests for blood group, hemoglobin, glucose levels, infections, and more to monitor the health of both the mother and the developing baby.

What is covered in a basic health check?
A basic health check usually includes blood tests, urine tests, and basic physical exams to assess overall health and detect any potential health issues early.
What specific conditions does the Senior Citizen Health Package screen for?
The package typically screens for chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, osteoporosis, and cognitive decline, ensuring a comprehensive assessment of an elderly individual's health.
What does a Renal Profile assess?
This profile evaluates kidney function through tests like creatinine, urea, and electrolyte levels to detect any renal issues early.

Health Check-Up Packages: Patient Instructions

• Report in the morning on an empty stomach
• Maintain overnight fasting for at least 10-12 hours
• Avoid coffee, tea, or milk except water
• After fasting blood sample, have regular breakfast
• Second blood sample (postprandial) should be given 1 1/2 – 2 hours after breakfast

Important Information:

• Ensure you are well-hydrated before the test.
• Avoid heavy meals before the test; light snacks are preferable.
• Inform the staff of any allergies or medical conditions.
• If the package includes TMT (Treadmill Test), do not take BP (blood pressure) tablets on the day of the test.
• Wear comfortable clothing and footwear suitable for exercise if undergoing a TMT.
• Male patients should remove chest hair for accurate test results.
• Bring all your previous medical reports, if any, when coming for consultation.
